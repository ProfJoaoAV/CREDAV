⚡ What is the Present Continuous?

We use the Present Continuous to talk about:

✅ Things happening right now (at this moment)

✅ Things happening around now (these days)

✅ Future plans already decided

Common signal words:
now right now at the moment today Look! Listen!
Look! He is running to school right now.
I am eating lunch at the canteen with my friends.
Are you dancing later today?

🔷 Affirmative Form

Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing
SubjectAuxiliaryExample
IamI am playing guitar.
He / She / ItisShe is painting a portrait.
You / We / TheyareThey are studying now.

🔴 Negative Form

Subject + am/is/are + not + verb-ing
They are not playing football. / They aren't playing football.
I am not doing my homework. / I 'm not doing my homework.
She is not watching TV. / She isn't watching TV.

❓ Interrogative Form

Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing?
Are you writing for the school newspaper?
Is Sam playing guitar at the moment?
Are they painting their portraits now?

💬 Short Answers

To answer Yes/No questions in the Present Continuous, use short answers:

Yes, + subject + am/is/are.
No, + subject + am/is/are + not.
QuestionYesNo
Am I...?Yes, you are.No, you aren't.
Is he/she/it...?Yes, he/she/it is.No, he/she/it isn't.
Are you...?Yes, I am.No, I 'm not.
Are they/we...?Yes, they/we are.No, they/we aren't.
Is she listening to music? → No, she isn't.
Are they playing football? → Yes, they are.
Is he playing the piano? → No, he isn't.

✏️ Spelling Rules for -ing

RuleBase Verb-ing form
Most verbs: just add -ingplay, eat, walkplaying, eating, walking
Verb ends in -e: remove -e, add -ingdance, take, writedancing, taking, writing
Short verb ends in consonant-vowel-consonant: double last letterrun, sit, swimrunning, sitting, swimming
Verb ends in -ie: change to -y + inglie, dielying, dying

⚡ From the test:

run → running (double n)
dance → dancing (remove e)
take → taking (remove e)
write → writing (remove e)
play → playing (just add -ing)
cook → cooking (just add -ing)
